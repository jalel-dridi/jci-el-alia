## 4. Top Flagship Projects

### Orient'Up (4+ editions: Orient'Up, 2.0, 3.0, 4.0)
- **Category**: Education / Career Guidance
- **Description**: Annual university orientation event helping baccalaureate students choose their academic path. Provides guidance on specializations and university options.
- **Recurring**: Yes — flagship annual project

### El Alia Champions Cup
- **Category**: Sports / Community
- **Description**: Multi-day sports tournament (football/handball) for local clubs. Includes opening ceremony, match days, and closing ceremony.
- **Evidence**: Photo albums for Day 1, Day 3, and Closing Ceremony (June 2024)

### إنطلاقة / Intalqa (Editions 1.0 & 2.0)
- **Category**: Entrepreneurship / Economic Empowerment
- **Description**: Project supporting small business owners and artisans. Includes training workshops in various specializations and a marketplace/exhibition for products and services. Aims to encourage local entrepreneurship.
- **Date**: February 7–8, 2026 (edition 2.0) at Maison des Associations

### Bac'Up
- **Category**: Education
- **Description**: Training program for baccalaureate students focused on practical skills (TP - Travaux Pratiques). Helps students improve their applied/practical exam performance through hands-on workshops led by specialists.
- **Recurring**: Yes (2025 and 2026 editions)

### Rising Kids (Editions 1 & 2)
- **Category**: Children / Youth Development
- **Description**: Program targeting children with educational and developmental activities.
- **Location**: Maison des Jeunes El Alia

### Success Stories 619
- **Category**: Inspiration / Community
- **Description**: Event celebrating Tunisian success stories. Speakers share their journeys and challenges to inspire local youth. Interactive format with audience engagement.
- **Date**: July 8, 2023

### InnoVenture Hackathon
- **Category**: Innovation / Entrepreneurship
- **Description**: Hackathon event fostering innovation and entrepreneurial thinking.
- **Date**: January 2025

### Voice Masters
- **Category**: Personal Development / Debate
- **Description**: Forum dedicated to debate and public speaking (Art Oratoire). Collaborative event with JCI El Alia, Ras Jebel, Metline, Rafraf, Zouaouine, and Ghar El Melh. Prepares members for debate championships.
- **Date**: March 11, 2026

### Talk Masters (2024)
- **Category**: Personal Development / Debate
- **Description**: Forum co-organized with JCI Ras Jebel and JCI Rafraf (Feb 17-18, 2024 at Maison des Jeunes Ras Jebel). Featured "Public Speaking" training by Brahim Ben Hassine and "Debating Techniques" by Mohamed Aouina (2022 Africa & Middle East Debating Champion). Sponsored by MediaPoint.

### Empowerment Exchange (2024)

## 9. Website Trilingual Support Requirement

**The website MUST support three languages:**

| Language | Direction | Priority |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| **Arabic (العربية)** | RTL | Primary (community audience) |
| **French (Français)** | LTR | Primary (institutional/official) |
| **English** | LTR | Secondary (international/JCI global) |

### Rationale:
- Content is naturally produced in all three languages
- Arabic is the community language (local audience in El Alia)
- French is the institutional language (JCI Tunisia official communications)
- English connects to JCI International and global branding
- Some posts are already trilingual (e.g., logo rebranding announcement)
- Hashtags span all three languages

### Implementation Notes:
- RTL support required for Arabic
- Content may not always have 1:1 translations — some content is language-native
- Tunisian dialect content should be categorized under Arabic
- JCI terminology should be consistent across languages (e.g., "Jeune Chambre Internationale" / "Junior Chamber International" / "الغرفة الفتية الاقتصادية العالمية")
